The Bioscience Challenge

In Malaysia, securing domestic rice supply is a cornerstone of national Food Security. The Padi MR297 variety (developed by MARDI) is optimized for its high yield, but reaching its genetic potential requires precise fertilizer timing.

By modeling dry plant biomass $f(t)$ in grams over a 120-day growth cycle, agronomic researchers apply derivatives $f'(t)$ to compute the Absolute Growth Rate (AGR). The peak of $f'(t)$ corresponds to the crop's vegetative transition, identifying the absolute critical window for nitrogen top-dressing.

Model adapted from: MARDI Padi Manual (MR297 Guideline) & Dept. of Agriculture Malaysia (DOA)

The Growth Derivative Model

CUMULATIVE BIOMASS FUNCTION:
$$f(t) = \frac{K}{1 + e^{-\beta (t - t_0)}}$$
Where $K$ = max yield biomass (g), $\beta$ = growth coefficient, $t_0$ = inflection day (days).
THE DERIVATIVE (GROWTH RATE):
$$f'(t) = \beta \cdot f(t) \cdot \left(1 - \frac{f(t)}{K}\right)$$
Calculates the instant rate of biomass change in $\text{g/day}$ at any specific day $t$.
